2. Scope of this policy

  • Prospects we discuss potential projects with;

It does not cover external sites we link to (Instagram, Facebook, Pinterest, X, etc.). When you click an outbound link you leave our site and the destination’s own policy applies.

3. Categories of data we process

3.1 Data you actively provide

  • Identification & contact data: name, business name, email address, WhatsApp number, any other detail you choose to send us.
  • Project content: the brief, files, screenshots, credentials, login URLs and reference material you share so we can do the work.
  • Correspondence: the contents of your emails, WhatsApp messages, voice notes and meeting notes.

3.2 Data collected automatically (browsing)

  • Always-on, anonymous, no consent required: aggregate, cookie-free traffic statistics via Cloudflare Web Analytics — page views, country, browser, Core Web Vitals (LCP, INP, CLS, TTFB), referring domain. No fingerprinting, no IP storage, no per-visitor profiling.
  • Local storage on your device, no consent required: two technical entries that never leave your browser:
    • customy.theme — remembers whether you chose dark or light mode;
    • customy.consent.v1 — remembers your choice in the cookie banner so we don’t ask again on every visit.
  • Only loaded after you accept the relevant category in the cookie banner — see section 5 below for the full list.

3.3 Data we do NOT collect

  • No payment data on this site (invoicing happens through your usual channel after a separate agreement).
  • No special category data within the meaning of Article 9 GDPR (health, biometrics, political opinions, etc.).
  • No data about minors — this site is not directed at children (see section 14).
  • No data sold to data brokers, advertising networks or similar third parties.

9. Security measures

Concrete safeguards we implement:

  • HTTPS / TLS 1.3 enforced on the entire domain via Cloudflare;
  • HTTP Strict Transport Security (HSTS) and a strict Content-Security-Policy on every page of this site;
  • Two-factor authentication enabled on every operational account (Google Workspace, Cloudflare, GitHub, hosting, project tools);
  • Password manager with unique passwords per account; no shared credentials;
  • Principle of least privilege: client credentials are only stored in encrypted vaults agreed with the client and deleted immediately at end of engagement;
  • End-to-end encryption for WhatsApp correspondence (provided by WhatsApp);
  • Encryption in transit and at rest for data held in Google Workspace.

If a personal data breach occurs and is likely to result in a risk to your rights and freedoms, we will notify the competent supervisory authority within 72 hours of becoming aware (Art. 33 GDPR) and, where the risk is high, we will inform affected individuals without undue delay (Art. 34 GDPR).

10. Your rights

Under the GDPR you have the following rights with respect to your personal data:

Right of access (Art. 15)
Obtain confirmation that we process data about you and a copy of that data.
Right to rectification (Art. 16)
Have inaccurate data corrected and incomplete data completed.
Right to erasure / “right to be forgotten” (Art. 17)
Have your data deleted where one of the grounds in Art. 17 applies (e.g. data no longer necessary, consent withdrawn).
Right to restriction of processing (Art. 18)
Ask us to freeze processing in specific situations (e.g. while accuracy is being verified).
Right to data portability (Art. 20)
Receive the data you provided in a structured, commonly used, machine-readable format and transmit it to another controller.
Right to object (Art. 21)
Object at any time to processing based on our legitimate interest, including profiling.
Right to withdraw consent (Art. 7)
Withdraw consent at any time, without affecting the lawfulness of processing already carried out on the basis of that consent.
Right to define post-mortem instructions (Art. 85 LIL)
Specifically for residents of France: leave general or specific instructions about what should happen to your data after your death.
